We have reached by transposition the Steinitz Variation of the QGA. It has been unpopular lately as it offers White a good version of an IQP position. He has not lost tempos on Bd3xc4 like in the Nimzo, nor on a4 (this comes in a set with a hole on b4), like in one of the main lines of the QGA. As a result, White usually achieves to relocate his queen to a more active place on the kingside and gains a lasting initiative.
